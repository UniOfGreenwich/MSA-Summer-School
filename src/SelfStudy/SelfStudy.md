# Self-Study: Review of Digital Twin (DT) Technology

As part of your self-study activity, you will be presenting your findings of a review of the above title and reflection on how the material covered during the preceding days relates to this topic. Although all groups will be working with this title, your review/presentation should deal with the specific application area allocated to your group (as will be detailed below).

The first part of the study is to conduct a thorough literature review and collect appropriate references from the literature (at least 15 references – you must use material from reliable sources/publications (IEEE Explore, Compendex, ScienceDirect etc - https://pages.gre.ac.uk/BannerExtensibility/customPage/page/userEresources ).

You should then prepare your power point presentation slides. The final part of the activity will be an in-person presentation of your findings on Wednesday of next week.

At the minimum, your presentation slides should cover the following:

1.	Front cover

2.	A brief introduction – to introduce yourselves and the topic highlighting your specific application area. (1 slide)

3.	Background: history and level of development/maturity of the technology; unique selling points of the technology and how it differs from modelling/simulation. (2 to 3 slides)

4.	Technical: what tools, devices, functions, skill sets etc are required to realise DT and how they all fit together. (2 to 3 slides)

5.	Reflective analysis: give a brief analysis of how the material you have learned/done during the preceding days relates to DT – justify your claims by siting examples of skills gained, tools used etc from your activities. (2 to 3 slides)

6.	Conclusion: summarise your presentation and briefly highlight how experience from summer school may impact your future decisions, direction etc (small to big). (1 to 2 slides)

7.	List of references (Use Harvard scheme of referencing). (Number of slides as required).

**Note that:** 

-	You will have 12 minutes to present followed by Q&A session for each group. 

-	You should have a maximum of 12 slides (excluding front page and references). 

-	Use the following minimum font sizes: 36 points for the page title, 32 points for headings and 28 points for normal text in slides.

-	Avoid use of too much text in your slides (use bullet points with short sentences/phrases to guide your delivery).

-	Group members must share both preparation and presentation of your findings equally. We will ask questions targeted at individual members of the group to gauge level of contribution.

**Application Areas** (each group should choose a unique application area from the list below):

-	Application 1: Rail Networks

-	Application 2: Electricity Grids

-	Application 3: Electric Vehicles

-	Application 4: Manufacturing Plants

-	Application 5: Retail

-	Application 6: Water Pumping station

-	Application 7: Smart Home
